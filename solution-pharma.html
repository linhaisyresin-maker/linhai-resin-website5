Antibiotic Extraction Workflow

Our macroporous adsorption resins (D-Series) are optimized for large molecule capture from fermentation broths.

STAGE 01

Filtration

Removal of mycelium and suspended solids from the fermentation broth.

STAGE 02

Adsorption

Fermentation broth passes through D-Series Macroporous Resin. Targeted molecules are captured.

KEY STAGE

Elution

Captured antibiotics are eluted using specialized solvent mixtures at optimized PH/Flow rates.

STAGE 04

Crystallization

Final concentration and crystallization of the high-purity active pharmaceutical ingredient (API).

Why Pore Size Distribution Matters?

Unlike water treatment, pharmaceutical separation involves large organic molecules. Standard resins often suffer from "internal clogging." Linhai's Controlled Porosity Technology ensures that the average pore size matches the target molecule's molecular weight, ensuring maximum effective surface area and rapid kinetics.

For sugar decolorization (LH-008FD), our resins are treated with a unique Cross-linking Stabilization process, making them resistant to the high temperatures and osmotic shocks common in industrial sugar mills.
